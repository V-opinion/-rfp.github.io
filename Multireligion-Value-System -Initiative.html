Replace Religious Symbols with ccTLDs

Promoting Equality and National Identity Over the EU, UN, and NATO

Across international organizations like the EU, UN, and NATO, flags often wave with religious symbols. While these symbols represent cultural histories, they might overshadow the principles of equality, neutrality, and the unique identity of each nation.

By replacing these religious symbols with country-specific ccTLDs (country code top-level domains), nations can emphasize their technological presence and maintain a secular, inclusive representation in global platforms.
